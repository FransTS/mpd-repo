## Purpose

Create effective professional email correspondence with appropriate tone, clear structure, and actionable outcomes.

---

## Email Structure

### Subject Line
- Specific and descriptive
- Under 50 characters
- Include action indicator if needed: `[ACTION REQUIRED]`, `[FYI]`, `[URGENT]`

### Opening
- Lead with purpose (1-2 sentences max)
- State why you're writing immediately
- Don't bury the ask

### Body
- One idea per paragraph
- Use bullets for lists (3+ items)
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences
- Use white space strategically

### Call to Action (CTA)
- Specific ask
- Clear deadline
- Make it easy to respond

### Closing
- Match formality to relationship
- Include next steps if applicable

## Tone Calibration

| Level | Greeting | Closing | Style |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| **Formal** | Dear Mr/Ms [Surname] | Kind regards | No contractions, full sentences |
| **Professional** | Hi [First Name] | Best regards | Contractions OK, direct |
| **Casual** | Hey [Name] | Cheers / Thanks | Relaxed, conversational |

### Tone Selection Guide

| Recipient | Suggested Tone |
|-----------|---------------|
| Board/C-Suite (first contact) | Formal |
| Board/C-Suite (established) | Professional |
| Colleagues | Professional |
| Team members | Professional/Casual |
| External clients (new) | Professional |
| External clients (familiar) | Professional |
| Friends/peers | Casual |

## Key Templates

### 1. Request Approval

```
Subject: [Approval Required] [Topic] by [Date]

Hi [Name],

I'm seeking your approval for [specific request].

**Summary:**
[2-3 sentence overview]

**Recommendation:**
[Clear recommendation with rationale]

**Required Action:**
Please [specific action] by [date/time].

[If questions, let me know / Happy to discuss]

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Quick Reference: Power Phrases

### Opening Lines
- "I'm writing to request..."
- "Following our conversation about..."
- "I wanted to share an update on..."
- "Quick question about..."

### Closing CTAs
- "Please confirm by [date]."
- "Let me know if you need anything else."
- "Would [date] work for a quick call?"
- "I'll proceed unless I hear otherwise by [date]."

### Phrases to Avoid
| Instead of... | Use... |
|---------------|--------|
| "I hope this email finds you well" | [Get to the point] |
| "Sorry to bother you" | "Quick question:" |
| "As per my last email" | "Following up on..." |
| "Please advise" | "Please let me know..." |
| "ASAP" | [Specific date/time] |
